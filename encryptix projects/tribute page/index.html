Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Introduction

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.

Timeline of Life

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954: Graduated from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli
  • 1955: Joined Madras Institute of Technology to study aerospace engineering
  • 1960: Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)
  • 1969: Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)
  • 2002: Became the 11th President of India
  • 2015: Passed away on July 27 in Shillong, Meghalaya

Achievements

  • Played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998
  • Developed the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects
  • Recipient of Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor
  • Authored several influential books including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds"
